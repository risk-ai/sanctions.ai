How do Fintechs Achieve Compliance?

To be compliant with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) and related regulations, U.S. banks must adhere to a number of anti-money laundering (AML) and countering the financing of terrorism (CFT) requirements. One of these requirements is to ensure that they are not doing business with entities or individuals who are on certain sanctions lists.

The primary sanctions lists that U.S. banks should screen against are those maintained by the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) of the U.S. Department of the Treasury. These lists include:

  • Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List (SDN List): This list includes individuals and entities that are sanctioned due to their involvement in activities such as terrorism, drug trafficking, or proliferation of weapons of mass destruction.
  • Sectoral Sanctions Identifications List (SSI List): Targets certain sectors of the Russian economy, such as financial services, energy, metals and mining, engineering, and defense and related materiel.
  • Foreign Sanctions Evaders List (FSE List): Includes foreign individuals and entities that have violated U.S. sanctions related to Syria or Iran and those who have facilitated deceptive transactions for persons subject to U.S. sanctions.
  • The List of Persons Identified as Blocked Solely Pursuant to Executive Order 13599 ("the 13599 List"): Contains names of persons identified as part of the Government of Iran or an Iranian financial institution.
  • Non-SDN Palestinian Legislative Council List (NS-PLC List): Contains individuals who are members of the Palestinian Legislative Council, and transactions with these individuals would be prohibited.
  • Non-SDN Menu-Based Sanctions List (NS-MBS List): Includes persons subject to certain non-blocking menu-based sanctions as provided under specific sanctions programs.
  • Non-SDN Communist Chinese Military Companies List (NS-CCMC List): Identifies entities determined to be Communist Chinese military companies operating directly or indirectly in the United States.

    • This is not an exhaustive list, and OFAC can implement new sanctions programs or make changes to existing lists. Therefore, U.S. banks should regularly check the OFAC website or other official resources for updates.

    In addition to OFAC lists, banks also perform customer due diligence (CDD) and adhere to other BSA/AML requirements like Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) and Currency Transaction Reports (CTRs).

    Always consult with a compliance expert or legal counsel to ensure that your institution is meeting all current requirements, as regulatory landscapes can change over time.
